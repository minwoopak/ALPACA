ALPACA (A Location-wise Proteome/transcriptome Abundance Comparative Analyzer)

ALPACA (A Location-wise Proteome/transcriptome Abundance Comparative Analyzer) is a web-based visual data mining system for subcellular location-specific knowledge mining from multi-omics data.
ALPACA allows researchers to visually compare transcriptomic and proteomic abundance of different cancer types in location-wise and biological function-specific fashion.
From the transcriptomic and proteomic abundance data of cancers, location enrichment distributions are calculated. ALPACA visually presents the location enrichment distributions of different cancers in pathway(s)-specific manner so that researchers can easily perceive the discrepancies of the abundance enrichment in a biological context of interest.

Step1. Data Selection

Select the omics types and cancer types of interest to compare.
One can either compare:
  • Proteomics of two different cancers
  • Transcriptomics of two different cancers
  • Transcriptomics and proteomics of a single cancer type

Step2. Pathway Selection

Once the datasets(cancer pair) are selected, the location enrichment (normalized average abundance of genes or proteins) are shown on the cell diagram via color variations.

The user can select one or more pathways to add biological context to the analysis.
Pathways can be sorted by the degree of discrepancies between the two cancers.
Once a pathway(s)(or a category of pathways) is/are selected, data is then filtered to include only the proteins that are related to the selected pathway(s).

Raw & Reset Button

Clicking the ‘Raw’ button would show the location enrichment values without normalization (average abundance values from the original gene expression or protein quantification data are shown)

‘Reset Parameters’ button would unselect the datasets and pathways and return to the default parameter settings view.

Step3. Presentation of Location-specific Heatmaps Observe location enrichment distribution discrepancies between the two cancers.P

The discrepancies of the two cancers are quantified by the Mean Squared Error (MSE) and kullback leibler divergence (KLD).

Note that pathways list can be sorted by MSE or KLD in descending order.

For better perception of the location distribution information, the same information is also presented by the barcharts right below the cell diagrams.

Step4. Identification of Potential Key Proteins

Examine proteins that may cause the similarity or dissimilarity of the location enrichments.

Click on a location of the cell diagram so that only the proteins localized to that location is shown in the table.
